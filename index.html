About

Sekolah Ilmuwan Minangkabau (SIM) or Minangkabau Scientist School, is an informal school located in West Sumatera, Indonesia. The aim of the school is to achieve education quality through upgrading the knowledge in science and capability in research. SIM has some trimester main tiered programs participated by selected students and teachers across West Sumatera to introduce the basic science in general and pursue research innovation by considering the values of local wisdom. In addition, SIM also conducts capacity building to address the current issues of education in the regions, through workshops and remote-mentoring programs. In partnership with Yayasan Baabussalam Hasdar, Gerakan Indonesia Emas, and Dinas Pendidikan Sumatera Barat, SIM believe that the good quality education is not only achieved by the common teaching and learning method; but also by improving critical thinking, scientific literature, and understanding good manners.

Our Program

Sekolah Ilmuwan Minangkabau (SIM) has encouraged quality education through: (1) Enhancing scientific literacy of high school students and teachers (2) Creating a supportive learning environment that can trigger lifelong sustainable learning. This project is held annually and is divided into three stages, namely SIM-1, SIM-2, and SIM-3. 

SIM-1: Introduction to basic scientific skills

Introduction to scientific skills: critical thingking, problem solving, innovation, and method to get idea through brainstorming

Learn more

SIM-2: Introduction to research and mentoring

Take deep dive into research by teaching ethics of scientific writing, literature studies, and research methods

Learn more

SIM-3: Introduction to Scientific Presentation

Introduction in how to analysis, draw research conclusion and presenting research results through report, poster, and presentations

Learn more

The three SIM projects are comprehensive projects that provide diverse knowledge and experiences to acquire 21st century skills, such as problem solving, critical thinking, good communication, and collaboration.
